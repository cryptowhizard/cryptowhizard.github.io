## Executive Summary
The crypto market advanced on broad-based gains with total market capitalization reaching $3.24T while Bitcoin dominance climbed to 57.1%. Small- and mid-cap altcoins led the scoreboard today — Jelly-My-Jelly (JELLYJELLY) topped gainers with a +31.75% move — but pronounced downside among individual names (Sad Coin -49.49%) underscores continuing idiosyncratic risk.

## Market Overview
The aggregate market cap sits at $3.24 trillion, reflecting renewed risk appetite across crypto markets. Bitcoin dominance is 57.1%, indicating that BTC continues to command the majority share of market value and is exerting meaningful influence on capital flows into and out of altcoins. Bitcoin is trading at $92,496 and Ethereum at $3,250.25 — prices that, combined with the dominance figure, suggest a market where institutional and retail flows remain materially anchored to the two largest assets.

- Bitcoin momentum: With BTC trading at $92,496 and dominance at 57.1%, the market shows persistent allocation to BTC; momentum appears positive but not uniformly bullish for all alts. Expect BTC-led regime shifts to affect altcoin correlations.
- Ethereum momentum: ETH at $3,250.25 suggests steady demand for smart-contract activity; watch for correlation between ETH moves and DeFi/L2 performance.

## Market Outlook — What to Watch
- Bitcoin dominance at 57.1%: A rising dominance suggests capital may rotate into BTC and away from smaller alts; a declining dominance would support broader alt rallies. Monitor this metric for clues on allocation shifts.
- Macro and regulatory headlines: Any macro risk-off, rate-related announcements, or regulatory developments could quickly change sentiment.
- DeFi metrics: Given DeFi’s relative strength, watch TVL and protocol flows for signs of sustainable adoption or fleeting yield-chasing.
- Token-specific events: Listings, liquidity migrations, burns, or governance decisions can trigger outsized moves in both directions for smaller tokens — maintain an events calendar.
- On-chain indicators: Tracking large transfers, exchange inflows/outflows, and active address trends will help validate whether moves are retail-driven or institutionally-backed.
