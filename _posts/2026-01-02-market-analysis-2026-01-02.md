## Executive Summary
Total crypto market capitalization stands at $3.12T with Bitcoin dominance at 57.3%, as broad risk-on flows favor higher-beta sectors today. Meme tokens led gains (meme sector +14.94%), with PEPE topping the leaderboard at +24.57%; conversely, small-cap illiquid names such as The American Dream (DREAM) saw steep declines of -21.01%.

## Market Overview
The aggregate market cap is $3.12 trillion, indicating a sizable investible base for risk assets. Bitcoin commands 57.3% dominance and is trading at $89,779, while Ethereum is at $3,061.6. These readings show Bitcoin continues to hold the majority share of market capitalization, reinforcing its role as the primary liquidity and risk gauge for the broader market. The environment today is characterized by strong appetite for speculative and thematic bets—particularly meme tokens and certain layer-1/AI plays—while a subset of low-liquidity names experienced outsized volatility to the downside.

## Top Performers
- Pepe (PEPE) led all gainers with a +24.57% move to $0.00000517. The meme sector’s outperformance (+14.94%) likely buoyed PEPE, reflecting elevated social engagement and risk-on positioning among retail traders.

Why they moved
- Concentrated retail flows and social momentum are the most probable drivers for the meme winners; these markets are highly sentiment-sensitive and react quickly to viral catalysts, listing rumors, or concentrated buy pressure.
- Low market-cap tokens amplify percentage moves due to thin liquidity and limited order-book depth.
- Some non-meme outperformers (e.g., RENDER) may be benefiting from sector rotation into infrastructure and GPU/cloud-related narratives, but confirmation requires volume and on-chain checks.
